Pozole

Description

My mom would make this green pozole soup with tender pork shoulder, tomatillos, and hominy. Radishes and cotija cheese are essential toppings! Lime on the side. I made this once at my job on a farm and we had so many green tomatoes. They worked as an excellent replacement for the tomatillos!

The recipe I'm using is from New York Times and I'm making it later this week. Actually, this is not a green pozole recipe...whatever too late. This is from Mark Bitman. We'll see how it compares to my mom's recipe...

Ingredients

  • 2 Tbsp neutral oil
  • 2 lbs ork shoulder, cut into 2-inch chunks
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4 dried guajillo peppers
  • 2 cups dried hominy, soaked for 8 hours
  • 2 Tbsp fresh oregano
  • Toppings!
    • Cotija cheese
    • Limes
    • Radishes
    • Cilantro
    • Salt!

Steps

  1. Put oil in a large saucepan over medium-high heat. When hot, add pork and onions and sprinkle with salt and pepper. Cook, stirring occasionally, until pork and onions are deeply browned, 15 to 20 minutes
  2. Meanwhile, pull off whatever stems you can fro the chiles; break them in half and pour or scrape out the seeds. More seeds = more heat! Yum. When pork and onions are browned, add chiles, hominy, oregano and cumin. Add water to cover everything by about an inch. Bring to a boil, then adjust heaet so the mixture simmers steadily. Cook stirring occasionally and adding more liquid if necessary, until pork and hominy are tender, at least 1 1/2 hours.
  3. If you like, fish out and discard chile pieces or chop them up and stir them back into the pot. Stir in garlic and cook a few minutes more; taste and adjust the seasoning. The mixture should be a little soupy. Serve with your toppings!
