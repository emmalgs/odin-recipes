Parchment Wrapped Trout

Description

This is a very simple recipe from my time at a sweet little french restaurant in Portland. The hardest part is actually the wrapping of the trout. You want a nice pouch that puffs up in a hot oven or near some hot coals if you're camping.

Ingredients

  • Rainbow trout, head and innards removed.
  • Neutral oil
  • Black pepper
  • Fresh rosemary
  • Parchment paper

Steps

  1. In a spice grinder, put about 2 Tbsp of black pepper and a large handful or two of rosemary, tender parts only.
  2. Grind until it makes a fine, spicy smelling powder. Season with a few pinches of salt.
  3. Combine roesmary and pepper mix with about a cup of oil. I use a blend olive oil (a mix of vegetable and olive oil). Mix well until it forms a clumpless oily sauce.
  4. Prepare your trout. Lay filetted trout flat. Cut out the spine and fins. You should have two pieces of trout now.
  5. Fold a piece of parchment paper measuring about 11x17 inches in half, hamburger style. Place a cleaned trout half along the folded line
  6. Spoon about 2 Tbsp of your oil mixture over the trout.
  7. Fold the parchment over the trout. Now form your pouch. Folding a little bit at a time, make your way around the edge of the trout, about 7 folds in all.
  8. Set your oven to broil. Or start a campfire! If using the campfire method, you should've started that a while ago...Anyways, place your little pouch of trout a few racks below the broiler. You want it hot, like 500F, but you don't want to start a parchment paper fire in your oven...
  9. It should take only about 3-5 minutes if your oven is hot enough to steam that sweet sweet trout!
  10. Serve immediately with a fresh salad and a glass of wine.
